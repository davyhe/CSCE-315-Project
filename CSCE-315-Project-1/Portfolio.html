    Self-Introduction

    As an international student coming from China, I look forward to high-quality education in the U.S. since the first I heard of it. Therefore, I transferred to Oakridge School, which is located in Arlington, Texas, and spent five years studying there. During those years, I fell in love with its beautiful natural environment and abundant activities. After graduating from high school, I spent half a year improving my English academic skills at Southern Methodist University. Then I got enrolled in my current school, the Kansas State University (KSU) majoring in Computer Engineering.

    I became interested in computer technologies when I had my first laptop at 13. My parents used to worry that I would be addicted to computer games since I was too young to be self-disciplined. However, compared to those games, I was more enthusiastic about the computer itself. I was curious how a computer could be so powerful and intelligent to finish a surprisingly large number of complicated works. Beginning with reading some books on the basic knowledge and operations of a computer, I gradually started to operate a computer by myself following instructive videos on YouTube. My biggest interest was in learning the latest specs and manufacturing techniques of the CPU and the GPU. Since then, I decided to major in Computer Science at university in the future. However, after finding that Computer Science is highly related to computer algorism and programming language, which were what I was unfamiliar with. I wanted a major that combines both algorism and hardware knowledge, and finally end up choosing Computer Engineering as my Major at KSU.

    After I finished taking "Intro Computer Engineering" and "Circuit Theory I", which cover basic control embedded systems and the ability to solve complex linear circuits diagrams, I became more confident in the learning of Computer Engineering. My hard work paid back by offering me two A in both of these two courses. I am grateful for what KSU has taught me. However, when I am digging into advanced courses, the options are quite limited, and I don't have many choices for the senior research project. Thus, I end up choosing Texas A&M as my trasnfer school, and enrolled in Fall 2020.
